Advanced Chemistry

1. Density is defined as...

    force/area
    weight/volume
    mass/volume
    mass/molarity
    energy/mass*temperature

Correct!

Incorrect. Try again.

Incorrect. The correct answer was: mass/volume

2. The scientist credited with the discovery of the nucleus (of the atom) is...

    Democritus
    J.J. Thompson
    Robert Millikan
    Ernest Rutherford
    Stephanie Bertenshaw

Correct!

Incorrect. Try again.

Incorrect. The correct answer was: Ernest Rutherford

3. In any chemical reaction that goes to completion, the reactant that gets used up is called the...

    Normal Reactant
    Excess Reactant
    Limiting Reactant
    Weird Reactant
    Cheap Reactant

Correct!

Incorrect. Try again.

Incorrect. The correct answer was: Limiting Reactant

4. In an endothermic reaction...

    An odd number of electrons get transferred
    An even number of electrons get transferred
    The change in enthalpy is positive
    The change in enthalpy is negative
    Hess's Law no longer applies

Correct!

Incorrect. Try again.

Incorrect. The correct answer was: The change in enthalpy is positive

5. Our atmosphere is primarily which gas?

    Hydrogen
    Helium
    Oxygen
    Carbon Dioxide
    Nitrogen

Correct!

Incorrect. Try again.

Incorrect. The correct answer was: Nitrogen

6. In a state of dynamic equilibrium...

    The forward reaction rate is higher than the reverse reaction rate
    The forward reaction rate is lower than the reverse reaction rate
    All reaction rates are 0
    The forward reaction rate is equivalent to the reverse reaction rate
    The chemical reaction goes to completion

Correct!

Incorrect. Try again.

Incorrect. The correct answer was: The forward reaction rate is equivalent to the reverse reaction rate

7. According to Erwin Schrodinger's quantum theory...

    Electrons are always in fixed locations within the orbital
    Electrons move around in the orbital at extremely fast speeds
    Electrons split into many pieces, occupying the entire orbital
    A single electron is everywhere in the orbital, with different probability densities
    \(s\) orbitals reign supreme over other orbitals

Correct!

Incorrect. Try again.

Incorrect. The correct answer was: A single electron is everywhere in the orbital, with different probability densities

8. A molecule with a central atom having 5 single bonds and 1 lone pair has what molecular geometry?

    Trigonal bipyramidal
    Trigonal planar
    Octahedral
    Square Planar
    Square Pyramidal

Correct!

Incorrect. Try again.

Incorrect. The correct answer was: Square Pyramidal

9. The product of the concentration of \(H_3O^+\) ions in an aqueous solution and the concentration of \(OH^-\) ions at \(25\) \(^{\circ} C\) is always...

    \(6.022 \times 10^{23}\)
    \(1\)
    \(0\)
    \(1 \times 10^{-14}\)
    \(6.626 \times 10^{-34}\)

Correct!

Incorrect. Try again.

Incorrect. The correct answer was: \(1 \times 10^{-14}\)

10. Why does vapor pressure decrease when solute is added to water?

    Osmotic pressure causes more liquid water to be present in the solution
    Water vapor is attracted to the solute
    The volume of the container spontaneously decreases
    Attractive forces between water vapor molecules decrease, making the gas more ideal
    None of the above

Correct!

Incorrect. Try again.

Incorrect. The correct answer was: None of the above
