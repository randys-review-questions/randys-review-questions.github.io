Randy's Review Questions

AP English Language and Composition

Based on content taught by Megumi Yamamoto, Eileen Wildermann, and Christina Esposito in the 2018-2019 school year.

1. What distinction is primarily drawn in The Things They Carried by Tim O'Brien?

    Vietnam War vs. World War II
    Story Truth vs. Actual Truth
    Rain vs. Snow
    Generals vs. Soldiers

Correct!

Incorrect. Try again.

Incorrect. The correct answer was: Story Truth vs. Actual Truth

2. Which two characters died in The Scarlet Letter by Nathaniel Hawthorne at around the same time?

    Hester Prynne
    Arthur Dimmesdale
    Nathaniel Hawthorne
    Roger Chillingworth

Correct!

Incorrect. Try again.

Incorrect. The correct answers were: Arthur Dimmesdale, Roger Chillingworth

3. What was the one lie that Elizabeth told in The Crucible by Arthur Miller?

    She was suspicious of John's affairs
    Rebecca Nurse was a perfectly sensible woman
    She did not like Reverend Hale's involvement in their business
    Abigail is Hester Prynne reincarnated
    Her husband, John, is not an adulterer

Correct!

Incorrect. Try again.

Incorrect. The correct answer was: Her husband, John, is not an adulterer

4. Who in Native Son by Richard Wright did Bigger intentionally kill?

    Jan
    Mary
    Buddy
    Bessie
    Max

Correct!

Incorrect. Try again.

Incorrect. The correct answer was: Bessie

5. According to Robert Waldinger, what is the key to an individual's happiness?

    Wealth
    Long lasting relationships
    Finding a hobby
    Appreciating the little things
    Not thinking about happiness

Correct!

Incorrect. Try again.

Incorrect. The correct answer was: Long lasting relationships

6. Which of the following is/are (a) rhetorical device(s)? Select all that apply.

    Litotes
    Apostrophe
    Irony
    Oxymoron

Correct!

Incorrect. Try again.

Incorrect. The correct answers were: Litotes, Apostrophe, Irony, Oxymoron

7. The "Red Herring" fallacy is...

    the use of something irrelevant and misleading to support the argument
    attacking people rather than ideas
    beginning with a general claim, then explaining details
    explaining details, then ending with a general conclusion
    circular reasoning

Correct!

Incorrect. Try again.

Incorrect. The correct answer was: the use of something irrelevant and misleading to support the argument

8. What is the minimum number of sources required for an effective synthesis essay?

    1
    2
    3
    4
    5

Correct!

Incorrect. Try again.

Incorrect. The correct answer was: 3

    Style Analysis Essay
    Argument Essay

Incorrect. Try again.

10. An op-ed is a _____ piece of writing that primarily expresses _______.
