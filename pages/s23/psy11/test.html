1. According to Urie Bronfenbrenner's bioecological model of child development, parental workplace policies on taking parental leave is considered to be part of which structural level?

    Microsystem
    Mesosystem
    Exosystem
    Macrosystem

Correct!

Incorrect. The correct answer was: Exosystem

2. All of the following would decrease an infant's risk of dying from Sudden Infant Death Syndrome (SIDs) EXCEPT...

    putting infants to sleep on their stomach.
    putting infants to sleep on a firm mattress.
    breast-feeding infants (as opposed to using pacifiers).
    None of the above would decrease the risk of SIDs.

Correct!

Incorrect. The correct answer was: putting infants to sleep on their stomach.

3. Which of the following most accurately describes the process of the habituation method?

    When shown two stimuli side-by-side, an infant showing a preference of one stimulus over the other indicates they can discriminate the two stimuli, but this does not imply that they like the stimulus that they have a preference of more.
    When shown two stimuli side-by-side, an infant showing a preference of one stimulus over the other indicates they can discriminate the two stimuli and like the stimulus that they have preference of more.
    When shown one stimulus over and over again, an infant will get bored of that stimulus and look at the stimulus for less and less time. If this pattern of boredom continues even when the stimulus changes to a different one, this implies that the infant can discriminate between the two stimuli.
    When shown one stimulus over and over again, an infant will get bored of that stimulus and look at the stimulus for less and less time. If this pattern of boredom continues even when the stimulus changes to a different one, this implies that the infant cannot discriminate between the two stimuli.

Correct!

Incorrect. The correct answer was: When shown one stimulus over and over again, an infant will get bored of that stimulus and look at the stimulus for less and less time. If this pattern of boredom continues even when the stimulus changes to a different one, this implies that the infant cannot discriminate between the two stimuli.

4. Which of the following depth cues develop the earliest for infants?

    Binocular cues
    Dynamic cues
    Monocular cues
    Pictoral cues

Correct!

Incorrect. The correct answer was: Dynamic cues

5. Suppose that a 12-month old infant named Bob observes an adult carrying a box opening a door with their feet. Another 12-month old infant named Sally observes a different adult with their hands free also opening a door with their feet. When imitating the adult they observed,...

Assume that the door handle is low enough where both Bob and Sally would be able to open the door with their feet.

    both Bob and Sally would try to open the door with their hands.
    Bob would try to open the door with his hands, whereas Sally would try to open the door with her feet.
    Sally would try to open the door with her hands, whereas Bob would try to open the door with his feet.
    both Bob and Sally would try to open the door with their feet.

Correct!

Incorrect. The correct answer was: Bob would try to open the door with his hands, whereas Sally would try to open the door with her feet.

6. The English language contains some irregular words that do not follow normal linguistic "rules." For example, "went" (not "goed") is the past tense of "go." Which of the following most accurately describes how infants learn those irregularities over the course of their development?

    The infant learns the "rule" first (saying "goed"), then memorizes irregularities (correcting "goed" to "went").
    The infant learns of specific instances of irregularities first (saying "went" instead of "goed"), then learns of the "rule" (use "-ed" for past tense) but immediately understands that irregularities (like "went") are exceptions to the "rule."
    The infant learns linguistic "rules" (use "-ed" for past tense) and irregularities (saying "went" instead of "goed") concurrently.
    The infant learns of specific instances of irregularities first (saying "went"), then learns of the "rule" (changing "went" to "goed"), then some time later learns to treat irregularities as exceptions to the "rule" (correcting "goed" back to "went").

Correct!

Incorrect. The correct answer was: The infant learns of specific instances of irregularities first (saying "went"), then learns of the "rule" (changing "went" to "goed"), then some time later learns to treat irregularities as exceptions to the "rule" (correcting "goed" back to "went").

7. Stage theories emphasize _____ in a child's development and are considered to be _____.

    continuity, qualitative
    continuity, quantitative
    discontinuity, qualitative
    discontinuity, quantitative

Incorrect. The correct answer was: discontinuity, qualitative

    I and III only
    II and III only

9. Because of childhood amnesia, children are unlikely to remember any experiences before _____ years of age.

    two
    three
    four
    five

Incorrect. The correct answer was: three

10. Sometimes, when infants are in a state of distress, they will lightly rub their hands together or suck their thumb to try to alleviate their distress. This is referred to as...

    co-regulation.
    self-comforting behaviors.
    self-distraction.
    All of the above

Correct!

Incorrect. The correct answer was: self-comforting behaviors.

11. James Marcia developed four groups describing an individual's identity status: identity diffusion, identity foreclosure, moratorium, and identity achievement. Emily is actively exploring identities but has not yet made a commitment to one. Which group would she fall under?

    Identity diffusion
    Identity foreclosure
    Moratorium
    Identity achievement

Correct!

Incorrect. The correct answer was: Moratorium

12. Robert Sternberg's theory of successful intelligence revolves around three main types of abilities, which include all of the following EXCEPT...

    naturalistic abilities.
    creative abilities.
    practical abilities.
    analytic abilities.

Correct!

Incorrect. The correct answer was: naturalistic abilities.

13. Tracey is a participant in Mary Ainsworth's Strange Situation Experiment. When their mother leaves the room, Tracey is noticeably distressed and acts angrily towards her when she returns. What is Tracey's attachment style?

    Secure attachment
    Insecure-resistant attachment
    Insecure-avoidant attachment
    Disorganized attachment

Correct!

Incorrect. The correct answer was: Insecure-resistant attachment

14. Which of the following parenting styles results in the most positive developmental outcomes?

    Authoritative
    Authoritarian
    Permissive
    Uninvolved

Correct!

Incorrect. The correct answer was: Authoritative

15. Older adults are _____, whereas younger adults are _____.

    more likely to remember positive images than negative images, just as likely to remember positive images as negative images
    more likely to remember positive images than negative images, more likely to remember negative images than positive images
    more likely to remember negative images than positive images, more likely to remember positive images than negative images
    more likely to remember negative images than positive images, just as likely to remember positive images as negative images

Correct!

Incorrect. The correct answer was: more likely to remember positive images than negative images, just as likely to remember positive images as negative images
