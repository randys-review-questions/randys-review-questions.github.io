Randy's Review Questions

BIO 13: Cells & Organisms

Based on content taught by Lauren Crowe and Ekaterina Mirkin in Fall 2020. 60% of this quiz is about lecture content, whereas the remaining 40% is about lab content.

You are currently in test mode. To switch to learn mode, click here.

1. You grow a large amount of untransformed E. coli cells on an LB agar plate containing ampicillin. After incubating it overnight, you would expect to see...

    a lawn.
    individual transformed colonies.
    individual untransformed colonies.
    nothing.

Correct!

Incorrect. The correct answer was: nothing.

2. Lysis solution contains _____ and _____, which dissolve the cell wall and cell membrane, respectively.

    lysozyme, detergent
    detergent, lysozyme
    lysozyme, amylase
    amylase, lysozyme

Correct!

Incorrect. The correct answer was: lysozyme, detergent

3. Beta-mercaptoethanol, a reducing agent, helps denature proteins in preparation for SDS-PAGE by...

    breaking hydrogen bonds in the tertiary structure.
    breaking hydrogen bonds in the secondary structure.
    breaking disulfide bonds.
    breaking peptide bonds.

Correct!

Incorrect. The correct answer was: breaking disulfide bonds.

4. Why is Taq polymerase (as opposed to human DNA polymerase) used in PCR?

    Human DNA Polymerase denatures due to the high pressure conditions necessary for PCR.
    Human DNA Polymerase denatures due to the high temperature conditions necessary for PCR.
    Human DNA Polymerase denatures due to the low temperature conditions necessary for PCR.
    Human DNA Polymerase does not elongate nucleotide strands efficiently enough.

Correct!

Incorrect. The correct answer was: Human DNA Polymerase denatures due to the high temperature conditions necessary for PCR.

5. What is necessary in order to approximate lengths of DNA fragments in gel electrophoresis?

    A restriction enzyme
    A ladder
    A QIAquick spin column
    A reducing agent

Correct!

Incorrect. The correct answer was: A ladder

6. When pipetting, the first stop is for _____, while the second stop is for _____.

    transferring liquid, cleaning out the pipette
    cleaning out the pipette, transferring liquid
    drawing liquid, expelling liquid
    expelling liquid, drawing liquid

Correct!

Incorrect. The correct answer was: drawing liquid, expelling liquid

7. All of the following are steps that one must take before using a centrifuge EXCEPT...

    ensuring that the lid of the centrifuge is closed.
    ensuring that the centrifuge is balanced, potentially by adding empty test tubes.
    ensuring that any open cap of a tube is opened toward the center of the centrifuge.
    setting the centrifuge to the correct time and rpm.

Correct!

Incorrect. The correct answer was: ensuring that any open cap of a tube is opened toward the center of the centrifuge.

8. Why won't cells of well-isolated haploid yeast colonies fertilize to form diploid cells?

    A fertilization promoting factor needs to be present for fertilization to occur.
    Well-isolated haploid colonies have undergone transformation, preventing fertilization from occurring.
    The cells hate each other too much to even consider combining with each other.
    There is a mechanism against genetically identical cells fertilizing.

Correct!

Incorrect. The correct answer was: There is a mechanism against genetically identical cells fertilizing.

9. Knowing the genotype of a haploid cell _____ allow one to unambiguously determine its phenotype. Knowing the genotype of a diploid cell _____ allow one to unambiguously determine its phenotype.

    can, can
    can, cannot
    cannot, can
    cannot, cannot

Correct!

Incorrect. The correct answer was: can, can

10. If there are more offspring with recombinant genotypes than offspring with parental genotypes, then...

    the genes in question are unlinked.
    the genes in question are unlinked if the difference is statistically significant, linked if not.
    the genes in question are linked if the difference is statistically significant, unlinked if not.
    the genes in question are linked.

Correct!

Incorrect. The correct answer was: the genes in question are unlinked.

11. Which of the following correctly lists three types of cells from smallest to largest?

    Prokaryotic Cells, Eukaryotic Animal Cells, Eukaryotic Plant Cells
    Eukaryotic Animal Cells, Eukaryotic Plant Cells, Prokaryotic Cells
    Eukaryotic Animal Cells, Prokaryotic Cells, Eukaryotic Plant Cells
    Prokaryotic Cells, Eukaryotic Plant Cells, Eukaryotic Animal Cells

Correct!

Incorrect. The correct answer was: Prokaryotic Cells, Eukaryotic Animal Cells, Eukaryotic Plant Cells

12. An amide group on an amino acid (with the absence of other functional groups) would make that amino acid...

    nonpolar
    uncharged polar
    acidic
    basic

Correct!

Incorrect. The correct answer was: uncharged polar

13. A hairpin structure would refer to RNA's _____ structure.

    primary
    secondary
    tertiary
    quaternary

Correct!

Incorrect. The correct answer was: secondary

14. Which of the following is a true statement regarding energy?

    Reactions that result in an increase in entropy are always spontaneous.
    Reactions that result in a decrease in enthalpy are exergonic.
    Longer, weaker bonds store more potential energy.
    Longer, weaker bonds require more energy to break.

Correct!

Incorrect. The correct answer was: Longer, weaker bonds store more potential energy.

15. The hydrolysis of sucrose is a(n) _____ reaction and it _____ energy.

    catabolic, absorbs
    catabolic, releases
    anabolic, absorbs
    anabolic, releases

Correct!

Incorrect. The correct answer was: catabolic, releases

16. Which complex of the electron transport chain (of the mitochondria) is NOT a proton pump?

    I
    II
    III
    IV

Correct!

Incorrect. The correct answer was: II

17. The light independent reactions of photosynthesis involve the conversion of ____ energy to ____ energy.

Note: Although chemical energy is technically a form of potential energy, for the purposes of this question, potential energy is defined by energy produced by an ion gradient (NOT referring to chemical energy).

    sunlight, chemical
    sunlight, potential (ion gradient)
    potential (ion gradient), chemical
    chemical, chemical

Correct!

Incorrect. The correct answer was: chemical, chemical

18. After anaphase but before cytokinesis, the cell has a total of how many chromosomes?

    23
    46
    92
    184

Correct!

Incorrect. The correct answer was: 92

19. After crossing two dihybrids, what percentage of the offspring will exhibit the dominant phenotype for both traits?

    12.50%
    25%
    50%
    56.25%

Correct!

Incorrect. The correct answer was: 56.25%

20. How is telomerase able to extend telomeres with the appropriate sequence?

    Telomerase contains a DNA template.
    Telomerase contains an RNA template.
    Telomerase contains amino acids that correspond to specific nucleotides.
    Telomerase contains carbohydrates that correspond to specific nucleotides.

Correct!

Incorrect. The correct answer was: Telomerase contains an RNA template.

21. Which type of mutation would lead to a dramatic shortening of the translated polypeptide chain?

    Silent mutation
    Missense mutation
    Frameshift mutation
    Nonsense mutation

Correct!

Incorrect. The correct answer was: Nonsense mutation

22. In eukaryotic regulation of gene expression, _____ bind to enhancers, while _____ bind to silencers.

    repressors, activators
    activators, repressors
    promoter-proximal elements, operators
    operators, promoter-proximal elements

Correct!

Incorrect. The correct answer was: activators, repressors

23. Introns are present in the _____, but not in the _____.

    transcriptome, proteome
    proteome, transcriptome
    transcriptome, genome
    genome, transcriptome

Correct!

Incorrect. The correct answer was: genome, transcriptome

24. _____ is a motor protein that moves toward the positive end of a microtubule, while _____ is a motor protein that moves toward the negative end.

    kinesin, dynein
    dynein, kinesin
    kinesin, myosin
    myosin, kinesin

Correct!

Incorrect. The correct answer was: kinesin, dynein

25. Cells on the intestinal wall are connected in a way that prevents fluid from seeping between them. They are probably connected by...

    a desmosome.
    a gap junction.
    a tight junction.
    plasmodesmata.

Correct!

Incorrect. The correct answer was: a tight junction.
