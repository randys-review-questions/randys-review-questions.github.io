1. Often times, when introducing a toxin at the lowest trophic level, it is the top-level carnivores that are the most negatively affected. This exhibits a phenomenon known as...

    Biological magnification
    Bioremediation
    Biological augmentation
    Resource partitioning
    Trophic efficiency

Correct!

Incorrect. The correct answer was: Biological magnification

2. Which of the following would diffuse through a cell membrane (without going through a transport protein) most easily?

    Small nonpolar molecule
    Large nonpolar molecule
    Small polar molecule
    Large polar molecule
    Small ion

Correct!

Incorrect. The correct answer was: Small nonpolar molecule

3. Nucleotides are synthesized via dehydration synthesis, forming a(n) ______ between adjacent nucleotides.

    glycosidic linkage
    peptide bond
    phosphodiester bond
    hydrolysis linkage
    hydrogen bond

Correct!

Incorrect. The correct answer was: phosphodiester bond

4. Which of the following is/are (a) function(s) of the smooth endoplasmic reticulum? Select all that apply.

    Lipid Synthesis
    Detoxification
    Calcium Ion Storage
    Protein Synthesis

Correct!

Incorrect. The correct answers were: Lipid Synthesis, Detoxification, Calcium Ion Storage

5. What important function does lactic acid fermentation execute when oxygen is not available?

    Oxygen is produced from breaking down pyruvate
    \(NADH\) is oxidized to \(NAD^+\)
    It maintains an \(H^+\) proton gradient in the mitochondrion intermembrane space
    It maintains an \(H^+\) proton gradient within the thylakoid space
    \(ATP\) is produced from the breakdown of pyruvate into lactate

Correct!

Incorrect. The correct answer was: \(NADH\) is oxidized to \(NAD^+\)

6. In which phase of meiosis are pairs of homologous chromosomes lined up on the metaphase plate?

    Prophase I
    Metaphase I
    Anaphase I
    Metaphase II
    Anaphase II

Correct!

Incorrect. The correct answer was: Metaphase I

7. Kobonozom (I made up that word) is a rare, X-linked dominant disorder. A male with Kobonozom marries a female without Kobonozom. Which of the following will be true of their children? Select two options.

    Each of their male children has a 50% chance of having Kobonozom
    None of their male children will have Kobonozom
    All of their female children will have Kobonozom
    None of their female children will have Kobonozom

Correct!

Incorrect. The correct answers were: None of their male children will have Kobonozom, All of their female children will have Kobonozom

8. Tryptophan, the product of a chemical reaction, can allosterically bind to a repressor protein, activating it and blocking transcription of an enzyme that catalyzes that reaction. Tryptophan, in this case, is a(n)...

    Corepressor
    Inducer
    Transcription Factor
    Activator
    Enhancer

Correct!

Incorrect. The correct answer was: Corepressor

9. In the process of gel electrophoresis, after DNA fragments are put into the well, what happens?

    The smaller fragments move toward the positive end; the larger fragments move toward the negative end
    The smaller fragments move toward the negative end; the larger fragments move toward the positive end
    All fragments move toward the positive end; the smaller fragments travel farther
    All fragments move toward the negative end; the larger fragments travel farther
    All fragments move toward the negative end; the smaller fragments travel farther

Correct!

Incorrect. The correct answer was: All fragments move toward the positive end; the smaller fragments travel farther

10. A trait that shows complete dominance is studied in a population in Hardy-Weinberg equilibrium. 64% exhibit the dominant phenotype. What is the allele frequency of the dominant allele?

    36%
    40%
    60%
    64%
    80%

Correct!

Incorrect. The correct answer was: 40%
