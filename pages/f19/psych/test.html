1. Which of the following is the only research method that can support a causal relationship between two variables?

    Case Study
    Naturalistic Observation
    Survey
    Correlational Study
    Experiment

Correct!

Incorrect. The correct answer was: Experiment

2. Bob just moved to a new town. He has trouble remembering his new address because his old address still comes to mind. This is an example of...

    Retrograde Amnesia
    Anterograde Amnesia
    Retroactive Interference
    Proactive Interference
    Negative Reinforcement

Correct!

Incorrect. The correct answer was: Proactive Interference

3. Broca's area, important for speaking, is located in which lobe?

    Left Frontal Lobe
    Left Parietal Lobe
    Left Temporal Lobe
    Right Frontal Lobe
    Right Temporal Lobe

Correct!

Incorrect. The correct answer was: Left Frontal Lobe

4. Different amplitudes of _____ waves are perceived as differing levels of _____. Select two options.

    light, brightness
    light, loudness
    sound, brightness
    sound, loudness

Correct!

Incorrect. The correct answers were: light, brightness, sound, loudness

5. Classical conditioning is most effective when the neutral stimulus occurs _____ the unconditioned stimulus.

    A long time before
    Immediately before
    At the same time as
    Immediately after
    A long time after

Correct!

Incorrect. The correct answer was: Immediately before

6. According to the Yerkes-Dodson law of arousal, a _____ level of arousal is preferable on easy tasks, while a _____ level of arousal is preferable on difficult tasks.

    lower, lower
    lower, higher
    higher, lower
    higher, higher
    None of the above

Correct!

Incorrect. The correct answer was: higher, lower

7. Which of the following psychologists is/are correctly matched with the area of development that they studied? Select all that apply.

    Jean Piaget - Cognitive Development
    Erik Erikson - Psychosocial Development
    Sigmund Freud - Psychosexual Development
    Lawrence Kohlberg - Moral Development

Correct!

Incorrect. The correct answers were: Jean Piaget - Cognitive Development, Erik Erikson - Psychosocial Development, Sigmund Freud - Psychosexual Development, Lawrence Kohlberg - Moral Development

8. Someone who is always willing to explore and try new things would score high on which Big Five personality trait?

    Conscientiousness
    Agreeableness
    Neuroticism
    Openness
    Extraversion

Correct!

Incorrect. The correct answer was: Openness

9. What percentage of WAIS test-takers would score within one standard deviation from the mean (85-115)?

    32
    34
    50
    68
    95

Correct!

Incorrect. The correct answer was: 68

10. What idea/conclusion did the results of the Stanley Milgram Experiment support?

    The brain waves of someone in REM sleep are very similar to those of one who is awake
    People tend to repeat reinforced behaviors; people do not tend to repeat punished behaviors
    A sense of power can cause people to get lost in their roles
    A majority of people would rather be wrong with the group than be correct alone
    A majority of people would fatally harm someone else if instructed to by an authority figure

Correct!

Incorrect. The correct answer was: A majority of people would fatally harm someone else if instructed to by an authority figure
