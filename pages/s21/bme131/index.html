BME 131: Principles of Medical Imaging

Based on content taught by Sergio Fantini in Spring 2021.

1. Marie and Pierre Curie, wife and husband, won the Nobel Prize for Physics in 1903, but only Marie won the Nobel Prize for Chemistry in 1911. Why did Pierre not also receive the Nobel Prize for Chemistry?

    Pierre's contribution was deemed not significant enough.
    Pierre focused entirely on physics, while Marie did a substantial amount of work in both Physics and Chemistry.
    Pierre tragically passed away between 1903 and 1911.
    Marie's just built different.

Correct!

Incorrect. Try again.

Incorrect. The correct answer was: Pierre tragically passed away between 1903 and 1911.

2. The pressure of ultrasound waves is continuous across the boundary between two media. Let \(p_i\) be the pressure of the wave incident on such a boundary, \(p_t\) be the pressure of the transmitted wave, and \(p_r\) be the pressure of the reflected wave. Assuming that the wave is propagating at an angle normal to the boundary, which of the following equations is true?

    \(p_i = p_t + p_r\)
    \(p_t = p_i + p_r\)
    \(p_r = p_i + p_t\)
    \(p_i + p_r + p_t = 0\)

Correct!

Incorrect. Try again.

Incorrect. The correct answer was: \(p_t = p_i + p_r\)

3. The purpose of the matching layer in an ultrasound transducer is to maximize transmission of ultrasound waves. What is the desired characteristic impedance of the matching layer?

    Extremely small (negligible)
    The characteristic impedance of the crystal
    The arithmetic mean of the characteristic impedance of the crystal and that of the tissue
    The geometric mean of the characteristic impedance of the crystal and that of the tissue

Correct!

Incorrect. Try again.

Incorrect. The correct answer was: The geometric mean of the characteristic impedance of the crystal and that of the tissue

12. All of the following are true statements about \(T_1\) and \(T_2\) relaxation in MRI EXCEPT...

    In general, \(T_2 < T_1\).
    \(T_1\) relaxation decreases when \(B_0\) (the large, static magnetic field) increases.

Incorrect. Try again.

Incorrect. The correct answer was: \(T_1\) relaxation decreases when \(B_0\) (the large, static magnetic field) increases.
