1. What is a conjecture?

    A statement that has been proven true.
    A statement that has yet to be proven or disproven.
    A statement that has been proven false.
    A statement that is not applicable to the scenario at hand.

Correct!

Incorrect. The correct answer was: A statement that has yet to be proven or disproven.

2. What is the contrapositive of "If I am at Tufts, then I am in Massachusetts"?

    If I am at Tufts, then I am not in Massachusetts.
    If I am not at Tufts, then I am not in Massachusetts.
    If I am in Massachusetts, then I am at Tufts.
    If I am not in Massachusetts, then I am not at Tufts.

Correct!

Incorrect. The correct answer was: If I am not in Massachusetts, then I am not at Tufts.

3. Which of the following is a tautology?

Assume that \(p\) and \(q\) are boolean values.

    \(p \to (\neg p)\)
    \((p \land q) \lor (p \lor q)\)
    \((p \lor q) \lor (\neg (p \land q))\)
    \(p \leftrightarrow q\)

Correct!

Incorrect. The correct answer was: \((p \lor q) \lor (\neg (p \land q))\)

4. Negate the following statement: For all \(x\), there exists a \(y\) such that \(p(x, y)\) is true.

    There exists an \(x\) such that for all \(y\), \(p(x, y)\) is true.
    There exists an \(x\) such that for all \(y\), \(p(x, y)\) is false.
    For all \(x\), there exists a \(y\) such that \(p(x, y)\) is false.
    For all \(x\), there does not exist a \(y\) such that \(p(x, y)\) is true.

Correct!

Incorrect. The correct answer was: There exists an \(x\) such that for all \(y\), \(p(x, y)\) is false.

5. Let \(A = \{1, 2, 3\}\) and \(B = \{3, 4, 5\}\). What is \(A \cup B \)?

    \(\{1, 2, 4, 5\}\)
    \(\{1, 2, 3, 4, 5\}\)
    \(\{1, 2\} \cup \{4, 5\}\)
    \(\{3\}\)

Correct!

Incorrect. The correct answer was: \(\{1, 2, 3, 4, 5\}\)

6. What quantity represents the number of size \(k\) subsets of a size \(n\) set?

    \(n^k\)
    \(k^n\)
    \({n \choose k}\)
    \({k \choose n}\)

Correct!

Incorrect. The correct answer was: \({n \choose k}\)

7. Let \(R\) be a relation on set \(A\), where \(R = \{(1, 1), (1, 2), (2, 1), (2, 2), (2, 3), (3, 2), (3, 3)\}\) and \(A = \{1, 2, 3\}\). Is \(R\) an equivalence relation?

    Yes
    No, because it is not symmetric.
    No, because it is not reflexive.
    No, because it is not transitive.

Correct!

Incorrect. The correct answer was: No, because it is not transitive.

8. An anagram of a word is a string of letters formed from reordering the letters in the original word. How many anagrams are there of the word FOOD?

For our purposes, a word is an anagram of itself.

    4
    12
    24
    256

Correct!

Incorrect. The correct answer was: 12

9. Proving a claim by mathematical induction is much like proving that all dominoes that are initially standing next to each other will fall. The base case involves proving that the first domino will fall. What does the inductive step involve?

    Proving that the second domino will also fall
    Proving that the last domino will fall in the same direction as the first domino
    Proving that the last domino will fall in the opposite direction as the first domino
    Proving that any domino falling will result in the next domino falling as well

Correct!

Incorrect. The correct answer was: Proving that any domino falling will result in the next domino falling as well

10. Let \(f\) be a function from \(A\) to \(B\). \(f\) is onto iff...

    \((a, b) \in f \land (a, c) \in f \to b = c\).
    \((a, c) \in f \land (b, c) \in f \to a = b\).
    the domain of \(f\) equals \(A\).
    the image of \(f\) equals \(B\).

Correct!

Incorrect. The correct answer was: the image of \(f\) equals \(B\).

11. By definition, \(a\) and \(n\) are relatively prime iff...

    \(a\) is prime and \(n\) is prime.
    \(a\) is prime or \(n\) is prime.
    \(gcd(a, n)\) equals either \(a\) or \(n\).
    \(gcd(a, n) = 1\).

Correct!

Incorrect. The correct answer was: \(gcd(a, n) = 1\).

12. Let \(p\) and \(q\) be really large prime numbers. Let \(n = pq\). What is the cardinality of the set containing all integers in mod \(n\) that are relatively prime to \(n\)?

    \(3\)
    \((p-1)(q-1)\)
    \(pq-2\)
    \(pq\)

Correct!

Incorrect. The correct answer was: \((p-1)(q-1)\)

13. Let \(C_n\) be a cycle graph with \(n\) vertices. Which graph has the largest maximum clique?

    \(C_3\)
    \(C_4\)
    \(C_6\)
    \(C_{10}\)

Correct!

Incorrect. The correct answer was: \(C_3\)

14. A perfect binary tree of height n has how many vertices?

    \(2^n\)
    \(2^{n-1}\)
    \(2^n - 1\)
    \(2^{n+1} - 1\)

Correct!

Incorrect. The correct answer was: \(2^{n+1} - 1\)

15. A graph is Eulerian (has an Eulerian tour) iff...

    all vertices have an odd degree.
    all vertices have an even degree.
    all but two vertices have an even degree; the remaining two vertices have an odd degree.
    either the second option or the third option is correct.

Correct!

Incorrect. The correct answer was: all vertices have an even degree.
