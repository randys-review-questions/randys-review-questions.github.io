Randy's Review Questions

PSY 13: Social Psychology

Based on content taught by Samuel Sommers in Fall 2020.

1. When analyzing research, all of the following are critical thinking "red flags" that would decrease the credibility of the research EXCEPT...

    jumping to conclusions.
    a conflict of interest.
    a statement of caveats.
    misleading data depiction.

Correct!

Incorrect. The correct answer was: a statement of caveats.

2. The Basic Dilemma of the Social Psychologist involves...

    the trade-off between effective research design and minimizing research costs.
    the decision on whether to make internal or external attributions based on people's actions.
    the trade-off between effective research design and meeting ethical standards.
    the trade-off between internal and external validity in research design.

Correct!

Incorrect. The correct answer was: the trade-off between internal and external validity in research design.

3. Susan walks by when Alice suddenly starts choking on a carrot. All of the following are potential barriers that may prevent Susan from helping Alice EXCEPT...

    Susan does not hold any religious beliefs.
    Susan and Alice are of different ethnic backgrounds.
    Other people are present, and they're not doing anything about it.
    Susan does not remember how to help someone who is choking.

Correct!

Incorrect. The correct answer was: Susan does not hold any religious beliefs.

4. Which of the following is an example of controlled thinking?

    The availability heuristic
    The representativeness heuristic
    Base rate information
    Counterfactual Thinking

Correct!

Incorrect. The correct answer was: Counterfactual Thinking

5. Your friend John is constantly being yelled at by his history teacher Mr. Fleming. But you notice that no other teacher ever yells at John, and Mr. Fleming yells at all other students equally as much. What kind of attribution would you make about Mr. Fleming and why?

    Internal attribution because consensus and distinctiveness are low
    Internal attribution because consensus and distinctiveness are high
    External attribution because consensus and distinctiveness are low
    External attribution because consensus and distinctiveness are high

Correct!

Incorrect. The correct answer was: Internal attribution because consensus and distinctiveness are low

6. Which of the following is a true statement about psychology in law?

    The testimony of a confident eyewitness is the most reliable evidence in court.
    A slow, thoughtful response when identifying suspects from a lineup is typically more accurate than a quick response.
    When presenting to a jury, it is in the lawyer's best interest to present evidence in chronological order as opposed to order of importance.
    A confession to a crime indicates guilt without a doubt.

Correct!

Incorrect. The correct answer was: When presenting to a jury, it is in the lawyer's best interest to present evidence in chronological order as opposed to order of importance.

7. Derek got a 74 on his math test, but he doesn't feel bad about it because his friend John got a 66. Derek is exercising...

    a fixed mindset.
    a growth mindset.
    upward social comparison.
    downward social comparison.

Correct!

Incorrect. The correct answer was: downward social comparison.

8. There are three fraternities at the University of College State. One requires no initiation to join, one requires a mild initiation, and one requires a severe initiation. The members of which fraternity would feel the closest together?

    The fraternity with no initiation
    The fraternity with a mild initiation
    The fraternity with a severe initiation
    There would be no significant difference between the fraternities

Correct!

Incorrect. The correct answer was: The fraternity with a severe initiation

9. If fear is used for persuasion, which of the following is essential?

    A large amount of fear is introduced.
    A moderate amount of fear is introduced.
    The message teaches the ability to reduce that fear.
    Both the second and third option are necessary.

Correct!

Incorrect. The correct answer was: Both the second and third option are necessary.

10. In Stanley Milgram's obedience study, participants were asked to incrementally increase the shock level of their "learners" by 15 Volts each time. The incremental nature of increasing the shock level is an example of...

    informational social influence.
    normative social influence.
    the foot-in-the-door technique.
    the door-in-the-face technique.

Correct!

Incorrect. The correct answer was: the foot-in-the-door technique.

11. The comments section of certain videos often contains sentences that the commenter would never say to anyone face-to-face. This is an example of...

    social facilitation.
    social loafing.
    deindividuation.
    groupthink.

Correct!

Incorrect. The correct answer was: deindividuation.

12. Across cultures and across species...

    older individuals tend to be more aggressive than younger individuals.
    male individuals tend to be more aggressive than female individuals.
    heterosexual individuals tend to be more aggressive than homosexual individuals.
    All of the above

Correct!

Incorrect. The correct answer was: male individuals tend to be more aggressive than female individuals.

13. All of the following are predictors of attraction to someone EXCEPT...

    similarity.
    complementarity.
    reciprocal liking.
    familiarity.

Correct!

Incorrect. The correct answer was: complementarity.

14. Which of the following would be the MOST effective in reducing prejudice between two groups?

    Explaining to each group positive characteristics of the other group.
    Having both groups attend a party together.
    Having the two groups compete in a game of dodgeball.
    Having the two groups work together to resolve a crisis affecting both of them.

Correct!

Incorrect. The correct answer was: Having the two groups work together to resolve a crisis affecting both of them.

15. Based on Robert Sternberg's triangular theory of love, the presence of intimacy and commitment but the absence of passion results in what kind of love?

    Romantic Love
    Companionate Love
    Fatuous Love
    Consummate Love

Correct!

Incorrect. The correct answer was: Companionate Love
